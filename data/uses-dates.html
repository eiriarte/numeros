Years

To say the year you simply name the corresponding cardinal number.

  • Estamos en mayo de dos mil diecinueve. — It’s May 2019.
  • Ana Comneno nació en mil ochenta y tres. — Anna Komnene was born in 1083.
  • Steve Jobs murió en el año dos mil once. — Steve Jobs died in the year 2011.
  • Nací en el setenta y cinco. — I was born in '75.

Although less frequent, it is also acceptable the form setiembre instead of septiembre.

Like the days of the week, the names of the months are masculine nouns, and are never capitalized:

  • Un frío (mes de) diciembre. — A cold December.

Dates

The elements of the date must be named in ascending order, that is: day + month + year.

For the day of the month we use normal cardinal numbers (dos, tres, cuatro, …, treinta y uno), except the first day of the month, which is usually expressed using an ordinal number (primero, instead of uno):

  • 1º de marzo de 2000 (primero de marzo de dos mil). — March 1, 2000.
  • 2 de enero de 1992 (dos de enero de mil novecientos noventa y dos). — January 2, 1992.

In Spain, though, the first day is also expressed with a cardinal: 1 de marzo (uno de marzo).

Shorthand notation

Very frequently, dates are written in abbreviated format, in "day + month + year" order. For example, 17 de abril de 2009 could be abbreviated as:

  • 17/4/2009
  • 17-4-2009
  • 17/4/09
  • 17-4-09
  • 17.4.2009
  • 17/04/2009 (not recommended)
